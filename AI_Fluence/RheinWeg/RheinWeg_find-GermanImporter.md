Channel 1: Your own network (aim for 3–5 conversations)
Warm intros convert 5–10x better than cold outreach. After 10 years in Germany plus 20+ years in IT, you almost certainly know people one step removed from importers: former colleagues whose companies buy hardware or components from Asia, parents at your son's school who run businesses, your Chinese community in the Rhein-Main area (some of them work in trading firms). The ask is simple: "Kennst du jemanden, der Waren aus China importiert? Ich mache gerade Interviews für ein neues Angebot und würde gern 20 Minuten mit so jemandem sprechen." Send that to 20 people this week.

Channel 2: LinkedIn (aim for 4–6 conversations)
This is your main cold channel, and it's where your Mittelstand segment lives.
Search filters: location Germany (or Hessen to start — meeting in person is a big trust advantage), titles like Einkauf, Einkäufer, Strategischer Einkauf, Leiter Beschaffung, Import Manager, Sourcing Manager, at companies with 10–200 employees. Also search keywords like "Import China" or "Sourcing Asien" in profiles. Expect roughly a 10–20% response rate, so contact 40–60 people to land 4–6 calls.
A connection note that works (personal, no pitch, explicit that you're not selling):

Guten Tag Herr/Frau X, ich baue gerade ein Angebot rund um die Verifizierung chinesischer Lieferanten auf und führe dazu Gespräche mit Einkäufern, die in China sourcen. Ich verkaufe nichts — ich möchte nur verstehen, wie Sie heute Lieferanten prüfen und was dabei schwierig ist. Hätten Sie 20 Minuten für ein kurzes Gespräch? Als Chinesin, die seit 2016 in Deutschland lebt, interessiert mich Ihre Praxis-Perspektive sehr.

The last sentence matters — your bicultural background is genuinely interesting to these people and lifts response rates.

Channel 3: FBA and e-commerce communities (aim for 3–5 conversations)
German-speaking groups exist and are active. German-language Facebook groups include Private Label Journey (FBA) and Amazon SEO (deutschsprachig), plus groups like "Amazon FBA Verkäufer Deutschland." Also look at the Reddit communities r/AmazonSeller and r/FulfillmentByAmazon, which focus on FBA logistics, sourcing, and fulfillment (English, but many German sellers there), and German podcasts/YouTube communities (Serious Sellers Podcast auf Deutsch, AMZ-Community-type Discords).
Tactic here is different: don't DM strangers cold. Instead, spend a week answering questions about supplier verification, Alibaba scams, and quality checks — you have real expertise. Then post one honest question: "Frage an alle, die in China sourcen: Wie prüft ihr einen neuen Lieferanten, bevor ihr Geld überweist? Seid ihr schon mal reingefallen?" That single post can generate several conversations, and the replies themselves are discovery data. Check each group's rules first; even research posts count as self-promotion in some groups if worded badly.
Channel 4: Local and offline (2–3 conversations, your unfair advantage)
You live 20 minutes from Messe Frankfurt. Ambiente, Tendence, and Christmasworld are full of German importers walking the halls — many exhibitors and visitors source from China. Also: IHK Frankfurt runs regular Außenwirtschaft/China events, and there are import/export and e-commerce Stammtische in Frankfurt (check Meetup.com and IHK's event calendar). A 10-minute face-to-face chat at an IHK event is worth five LinkedIn calls, and it's completely outside any cold-outreach legal questions.
Running the conversations
Keep a simple tracking sheet (name, company, segment, channel, status, key quotes). In each call, ask and mostly listen:

Wie finden Sie heute neue Lieferanten in China?
Wie prüfen Sie einen Lieferanten, bevor Sie die erste Zahlung leisten? (Schritt für Schritt)
Sind Sie oder jemand in Ihrem Umfeld schon einmal betrogen worden oder hat schlechte Ware erhalten? Was hat das gekostet?
Haben Sie je einen Verifizierungsdienst genutzt? Warum (nicht)? Was hat er gekostet?
Was wäre Ihnen eine gründliche Prüfung durch jemanden vor Ort in Deutschland wert?

Don't pitch until the end — and only if they ask. If they show interest, that's when the sample verification report (your other open action item) becomes the follow-up.
A realistic 3-week plan
Week 1: send 20 warm-network messages, 20 LinkedIn requests, join 3–4 communities and start answering questions. Week 2: another 20–30 LinkedIn requests, post your discovery question in one community, book calls. Week 3: conduct calls, attend one IHK/Meetup event, synthesize what you heard. By the end you'll know which segment (FBA sellers vs. Mittelstand importers) feels the pain more acutely — which answers the other open question in your context document.
